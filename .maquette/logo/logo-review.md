## Inspection

The sheet follows the approved brand language: graphite steel, paper-like drafting surface, restrained ferric oxide accent, measured construction lines, and industrial geometry. It is an exploration sheet, not a final logo asset.

## Recommended Direction

The strongest direction is **5. Girder F / bold**.

Reasons:

- It has the clearest silhouette at favicon and documentation-sidebar sizes.
- It can be expressed as an SVG with simple beams, diagonal braces, rivets, and one ferric node/accent.
- It keeps the industrial-strength idea without needing a full bridge scene.
- It is less likely than the bridge concepts to become overly detailed or construction-company-like.

## Secondary Direction

**6. Bridge Join / small** is the best supporting mark or icon-family idea. It reads more directly as a rule-network join, but it may become generic if used as the primary logo.

## Lower-Priority Directions

- **1. Girder F** has useful detail but is too tall and busy for small sizes.
- **2. Bridge Join** carries the bascule bridge metaphor but likely needs too much geometry at logo scale.
- **3. Drafting Arc** is attractive as a page motif but not distinctive enough as a primary mark.
- **4. Floorplan Network** is conceptually aligned but too detailed and static for a small mark.
