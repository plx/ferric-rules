# ferric-rules Logo Brief

This is a planning brief for logo exploration after the Maquette brand board is approved. The approved brand board will define palette, type direction, radius, surface treatment, and interaction language; the logo should then use those decisions instead of creating a separate identity system.

## Constraints

- Vector-first mark, suitable for SVG.
- Readable at favicon scale.
- Works in one color, reversed on dark surfaces, and with one ferric/rust accent.
- Avoid literal humanoid, mascot, seal, badge, or decorative crest treatments.
- Avoid looking like a generic Rust crate gear, construction-company logo, or blockchain/network mark.

## Concept Families

1. **Bascule Bridge Join**
   Two lifted bridge leaves become rule paths converging on a central join node. The geometry references movable bridges and Rete joins without becoming a literal diagram.

2. **Girder F**
   A compact steel-girder assembly forms a subtle `F` through negative space and diagonal bracing. This is the strongest candidate for small-scale recognition.

3. **Drafting Arc**
   A structural beam or rule path crosses a fine compass/protractor arc. Works well as a supporting brand motif and potentially as a logo if simplified aggressively.

4. **Floorplan Network**
   An L-shaped architectural plan corner contains two or three rule nodes and a join edge. This emphasizes embedding and rigor, but may become too detailed at small sizes.

## Preferred First Exploration

Start with the **Girder F** and **Bascule Bridge Join** concepts. They are the most likely to remain legible at small sizes while carrying the industrial/rules-engine metaphor.
